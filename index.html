H. Cumaili

Independent Researcher in Biblical Studies & Early Christian Interpretation

H. Cumaili is an independent researcher specializing in Biblical Studies, with a focus on the Hebrew Bible, Second Temple Judaism, and early Christian interpretive traditions. His work employs a textual-historical approach, examining how prophetic texts were formed, transmitted, and interpreted across time.

Research Focus

  • Johannine Literature and early Christian texts
  • Prophetic expectations in Second Temple Judaism
  • Historical reception of Paraclete passages
  • Textual and historical criticism of biblical texts
  • Development of theological concepts through textual layers

Research Statement

This research does not address the essence or nature of the Divine. Rather, it focuses on how the Divine is presented within the textual record.

The aim is to approach religious texts through disciplined textual analysis, prioritizing the integrity of the text as it stands, rather than later theological constructions.

The text comes first.

I write and the reader judges.
